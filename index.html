Research Thesis

Introduction to Rock Music Pedagogy

The integration of rock music into high school education represents a significant shift in pedagogical approaches to music instruction. This study examines the potential benefits and challenges of incorporating contemporary music genres into formal education settings.

Traditional music education has often focused on classical repertoire, but the 21st century classroom demands engagement with diverse musical forms that resonate with students' cultural experiences.

Research Question:

How does the incorporation of rock music in high school curricula affect student engagement, academic performance, and musical literacy?

Methodology and Data Collection

This mixed-methods study employed both quantitative and qualitative approaches to assess the impact of rock-based music instruction across five high schools in diverse socioeconomic settings.

Participants:

  • 120 students aged 14-18
  • 8 music educators
  • 5 school administrators

Instruments:

  • Pre/post musical knowledge assessments
  • Classroom observation protocols
  • Student & educator interviews

Findings and Implications

Preliminary results indicate significant improvements in student engagement and musical literacy when rock music is integrated into the curriculum. The following key findings emerged from the data analysis:

Key Findings:

Increased Engagement

85% of students reported higher motivation to attend music classes when rock repertoire was included

Musical Literacy

Students demonstrated 40% greater retention of music theory concepts when taught through rock examples

Social Benefits

Participation in rock ensembles improved peer relationships and school connectedness

Findings and Implications

Preliminary results indicate significant improvements in student engagement and musical literacy when rock music is integrated into the curriculum. The following key findings emerged from the data analysis:

Key Findings:

Increased Engagement

85% of students reported higher motivation to attend music classes when rock repertoire was included

Musical Literacy

Students demonstrated 40% greater retention of music theory concepts when taught through rock examples

Social Benefits

Participation in rock ensembles improved peer relationships and school connectedness
